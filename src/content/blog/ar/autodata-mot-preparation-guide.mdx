<h2>Why MOT Preparation Benefits from a Data Platform</h2>

<p>MOT testing is a standardised assessment against published criteria. The DVSA's <a href="https://www.gov.uk/guidance/mot-inspection-manual-for-private-passenger-and-light-commercial-vehicles" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">MOT Inspection Manual</a> sets out exactly what is tested and what the pass/fail thresholds are. AutoData provides the vehicle-specific technical data that maps to these thresholds:</p>

<ul>
<li>Brake efficiency calculations for the vehicle's specific test weight</li>
<li>Minimum tyre tread depth requirements (standard 1.6mm) and load/speed index requirements</li>
<li>Lamp specifications and type codes where relevant to functionality checks</li>
<li>Wheel fastener torque values (relevant to recent wheel safety protocols)</li>
<li>Emission limits for the vehicle type and registration date</li>
<li>Fuel system and exhaust specifications relevant to emissions testing</li>
</ul>

<h2>AutoData MOT Prep: Key Features</h2>

<h3>Brake Specifications</h3>
<p>AutoData provides: disc thickness (minimum and nominal), drum diameter (maximum and nominal), brake lining thickness minimum, and brake fluid specification. Cross-referencing a vehicle's actual disc thickness against AutoData's minimum specification is a 60-second check that prevents both advisory and failure.</p>

<h3>Service and Fluid Specifications</h3>
<p>A vehicle that hasn't had a correct oil specification or coolant inhibitor concentration could face issues with excessive emissions or cooling passages. AutoData's service specifications let you verify the vehicle's service history plausibility — and identify whether the service interval data indicates the vehicle is overdue maintenance that may affect emissions.</p>

<h3>Timing and Emissions Data</h3>
<p>Diesel vehicles with DPF issues frequently fail on opacity. But equally, a vehicle with an incorrect engine oil specification (too low viscosity) can show elevated particulate output. AutoData's oil specification data for diesel engines, including approvals (ACEA C3, etc.), helps identify whether the vehicle has been running an incompatible oil that could affect emissions test results.</p>

<h3>Suspension Data</h3>
<p>While suspension failure points depend on physical inspection, AutoData's technical dimensions (wheel geometry specifications) help identify whether a vehicle was presented within specification for alignment — and whether a customer-reported handling issue corresponds to a likely MOT advisory on a steering or suspension component.</p>

<h2>Step-by-Step: Pre-MOT Check Using AutoData</h2>

<ol>
<li><strong>Vehicle intake</strong> — Collect customer history, mileage, and any known complaints that may correlate with MOT advisory categories.</li>
<li><strong>Log the vehicle in AutoData</strong> — Enter make, model, year, engine variant. Access the Maintenance and Technical Data sections.</li>
<li><strong>Print brake specifications</strong> — Minimum disc thickness, drum diameter limits. Check actual disc thickness with a micrometer or calliper. Flag anything within 1mm of minimum as advisory.</li>
<li><strong>Tyre assessment</strong> — Check tread depth at minimum 3 points across the tyre width in the MOT test area. Check tyre sidewall condition. Verify tyre specification matches the vehicle's requirement (load/speed index) using AutoData's tyre specification data.</li>
<li><strong>Lamp check</strong> — Check all required lamps are functional. AutoData's electrical section confirms correct lamp specifications where substitution may have been attempted.</li>
<li><strong>Emissions pre-check</strong> — For petrol: check the CAT is working (scan for P0420). For diesel: check the DPF is intact and not removed (scan for related DTCs; check for regen history). Access AutoData's emission specification to understand the test thresholds for the vehicle's registration date.</li>
<li><strong>Full visual underside inspection</strong> — Assess: wheel bearing play, ball joint wear, tie rod wear, shock absorber condition, subframe corrosion, brake pipe condition. AutoData's technical dimensions provide context for borderline tolerances.</li>
<li><strong>Service indicator check</strong> — Confirm the service indicator is not illuminated. If it is, check AutoData for the reset procedure and service interval to advise the customer.</li>
<li><strong>Pre-MOT customer report</strong> — Present findings to the customer with the AutoData specification references for credibility. Obtain authorisation for remedial work before testing.</li>
</ol>
