<h2>What "Auto Repair Software" Actually Means for Small Shops</h2>

<p>Let's be clear about what we're discussing. This guide covers <strong>repair data software</strong> — OEM technical documentation, wiring diagrams, service intervals, diagnostic procedures, and TSBs. This is distinct from:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Workshop management systems</strong> (job cards, invoicing, customer records) — separate products like Garage Hive, Autowork Online</li>
<li><strong>Diagnostic scanner software</strong> (Autel MaxiSys, Launch X431) — reads live data and fault codes</li>
<li><strong>Parts-ordering systems</strong> (Aldoc, TecDoc) — parts catalogues and ordering</li>
</ul>
<p>The repair data software category — what ALLDATA, AutoData, HaynesPro, and Mitchell1 provide — is the reference documentation layer. Your scanner tells you <em>what</em> is faulty. Your repair data tells you <em>how</em> to test, confirm, and fix it correctly.</p>

<h2>Five Things Small Shops Should Look for in Repair Software</h2>

<h3>1. Vehicle Coverage Matching Your Vehicle Mix</h3>
<p>The best platform in the world is useless if it doesn't cover your most common makes. Before subscribing, test data coverage specifically for your top 10 makes by querying a complex current vehicle (not a 2005 model) for ADAS procedures or a complex electrical fault.</p>

<h3>2. Wiring Diagram Quality</h3>
<p>Electrical diagnosis is where small shops lose the most time. If the platform you're evaluating has poor wiring data — missing connectors, no component location, no pin-out views — budget time for a second subscription or move to a platform that covers this well. Access <a href="https://www.autofixdata.net/wiring-diagrams">interactive wiring diagrams</a> to see what professional-grade circuit data looks like.</p>

<h3>3. TSB and Recall Database</h3>
<p>A TSB database prevents unnecessary work. Every time you chase an electrical gremlins for two hours and then find a published manufacturer fix that a software update would have resolved, that's lost revenue. A TSB search should be the first step in every diagnosis.</p>

<h3>4. Total Cost of Ownership</h3>
<p>Factor in <em>all</em> subscriptions you'd need for complete coverage. A £65/month platform that covers 70% of your vehicles may require a £99/month supplement for the remaining 30% — making the effective total £164/month versus a bundled solution at £99–£140/month for 100% coverage.</p>

<h3>5. Usability and Training Time</h3>
<p>If a platform is complex to use, technician time is diverted from billable work to navigation frustration. Platforms with clean interfaces and consistent search conventions reduce training time and lookup friction — significant for small teams where every hour counts.</p>

<h3>Do small workshops actually need OEM repair data or is a scanner enough?</h3>
