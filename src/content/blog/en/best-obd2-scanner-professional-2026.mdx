<h2>Why Your Choice of OBD2 Scanner Matters More Than You Think</h2>

<p>A scanner that only reads basic OBD2 emissions codes is not a professional workshop scanner — it's a code reader. The difference is enormous:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Basic OBD2 readers (£30–£200)</strong>: Read and clear engine management codes from the OBD port. Cannot access Body, Chassis, or Transmission control modules. Not suitable for professional diagnosis.</li>
<li><strong>Entry-to-mid professional scanners (£500–£1,500)</strong>: All-system scanning of most common makes, guided procedures, actuation tests. Suitable for independent workshops handling mainstream vehicles.</li>
<li><strong>Professional mid-range scanners (£1,500–£3,500)</strong>: Comprehensive all-system scan, ADAS calibration functions, immobiliser and key programming, coding functions for module replacement. Necessary for full-scope independent workshop.</li>
<li><strong>Premium professional scanners (£3,500–£7,500+)</strong>: OEM-equivalent coverage, advanced programming, bi-directional control. Snap-on territory — outstanding capability at significant cost.</li>
</ul>

These come from your repair data subscription, not your scanner.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Is a £200 OBD2 scanner good enough for a professional workshop?</h3>
<p>No. For professional workshop use, you need an all-system scanner that accesses every control module on the vehicle — not just the engine management system. Budget a minimum of £700–£800 for a tool that will genuinely serve a professional workshop.</p>

<h3>How do I know if an OBD2 scanner covers a specific vehicle?</h3>
<p>Before purchasing, check the manufacturer's vehicle compatibility list for the specific make, model, and year of the vehicles you service most commonly. Pay particular attention to "All Systems" coverage versus "OBD2 Emissions Only" listings.</p>

<h3>Do professional scanners work on electric vehicles?</h3>
<p>Most professional scanners (Autel MaxiSys, Launch X431 Pro5, Snap-on ZEUS) include EV-specific modules for accessing battery management, inverter, and OBC systems. Check manufacturer specifications — not all EV makes are covered equally by all scanner brands.</p>

<h3>What is the difference between Autel and Snap-on scanners?</h3>
<p>Autel provides comparable functional coverage to Snap-on at approximately 30–50% of the price. Snap-on offers more refined user experience, better integration with Mitchell1 data, and premium support — but at a significantly higher total cost of ownership. Most independent workshops find Autel's MaxiSys range to be the better value proposition.</p>

<h3>Is Launch X431 as good as Autel MaxiSys?</h3>
<p>Launch offers very competitive coverage at a lower price point with good European vehicle depth. Autel MaxiSys has a broader feature set for advanced functions (coding, ADAS) and is generally regarded as having a more intuitive interface. Both are legitimate professional tools for different budget levels.</p>
