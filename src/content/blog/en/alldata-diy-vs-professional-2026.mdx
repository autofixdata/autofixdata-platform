<h2>What Is ALLDATA DIY?</h2>

<p>ALLDATA DIY is a consumer-facing product that gives vehicle owners access to OEM repair information for a single vehicle at a time. It's designed for home mechanics who want factory-level data without the full professional subscription cost. The platform includes basic repair procedures, maintenance schedules, and some wiring diagrams — but the depth is noticeably reduced compared to the professional tier.</p>

<h2>Why Professional Workshops Should Never Use ALLDATA DIY</h2>

<p>The appeal is understandable — £20/month vs £100/month is an obvious cost saving. But the economics fall apart immediately in a professional setting:</p>

<ol>
<li><strong>VIN-locked access</strong> — You'd need a new subscription for every different vehicle. At 10+ vehicles per day across different makes, that's £200–£300/month in DIY subscriptions vs £99/month for unlimited professional access.</li>
<li><strong>Reduced data depth</strong> — ALLDATA DIY deliberately removes complex diagnostic procedures, advanced wiring, and many TSBs. When you're diagnosing an intermittent fault on a 2022 BMW, the missing data costs you hours of diagnostic time.</li>
<li><strong>No multi-user support</strong> — A workshop with two technicians and a service advisor cannot share a DIY login. The professional tier supports concurrent sessions.</li>
<li><strong>No integration</strong> — ALLDATA DIY doesn't integrate with shop management systems or diagnostic scanners.</li>
</ol>
