<h1>Who Owns ALLDATA? The AutoZone Connection and What It Means for Your Workshop</h1>

<p>If you've ever searched for "who owns ALLDATA" or "ALLDATA Corporation," you've likely hit a wall of corporate jargon and contradictory information. The answer matters more than you might think — because the ownership structure of your repair data provider directly impacts pricing, data neutrality, and long-term platform development. Here's the full picture.</p>

<div style={{background:"#fff8e1", borderLeft:"4px solid #f4b400", padding:"16px 20px", borderRadius:"6px", margin:"24px 0"}}>

<strong>⚡ Quick Answer</strong><br/>
ALLDATA LLC is a wholly-owned subsidiary of <a href="https://www.autozone.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">AutoZone, Inc.</a> — the largest retailer of aftermarket automotive parts in the United States, with over 7,000 stores. AutoZone acquired ALLDATA in 1996 for approximately $95 million.

</div>

<h2>ALLDATA's History: From Startup to AutoZone Subsidiary</h2>

<p>ALLDATA was founded in 1986 in Sacramento, California, as one of the first companies to digitise OEM automotive repair information. In the late 1980s and early 1990s, ALLDATA distributed repair data on CD-ROMs — a revolutionary concept when most workshops relied on paper manuals from Chilton, Haynes, and Mitchell.</p>

<p>In 1996, AutoZone acquired ALLDATA to strengthen its professional business segment. The acquisition gave AutoZone a direct relationship with workshop professionals — a valuable channel for parts sales and wholesale accounts. Since then, ALLDATA has operated as a subsidiary within AutoZone's corporate structure, with headquarters remaining in Elk Grove, California.</p>

<h2>What ALLDATA Corporation Actually Is</h2>

<p>Despite the name recognition, ALLDATA is not an independent company. Key facts:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Legal entity:</strong> ALLDATA LLC, a California-based limited liability company</li>
<li><strong>Parent company:</strong> AutoZone, Inc. (NYSE: AZO), headquartered in Memphis, Tennessee</li>
<li><strong>Revenue contribution:</strong> ALLDATA's revenue is reported within AutoZone's "Other Business" segment — not broken out separately in SEC filings</li>
<li><strong>Employee count:</strong> Approximately 800–1,000 employees dedicated to ALLDATA operations</li>
<li><strong>Data coverage:</strong> Direct OEM licensing agreements with 44+ vehicle manufacturers covering 38,000+ vehicle applications</li>
</ul>

<h2>How AutoZone Ownership Affects Your Workshop</h2>

<h3>Pricing Considerations</h3>
<p>AutoZone's retail scale gives ALLDATA significant negotiating leverage with OEMs for data licensing. This theoretically keeps ALLDATA's pricing competitive. However, as a subsidiary of a publicly traded company, ALLDATA also faces pressure to maintain margins — which limits how aggressively they can discount.</p>

<p>ALLDATA Repair currently costs approximately £99–£110/month for single-user access. For comparison, <a href="https://www.workshopdata.us/pricing">AutoFixData offers five platforms including ALLDATA for £99–£140/month</a>.</p>

<h3>Data Neutrality</h3>
<p>ALLDATA maintains strict data neutrality — they license and reproduce OEM data verbatim without editorial modification. The AutoZone connection does not influence which repair procedures are shown or how they're presented. This is a genuine strength: ALLDATA data is factory-original, unedited OEM content.</p>

<h3>Parts Integration</h3>
<p>ALLDATA Repair integrates with AutoZone's parts catalogue, allowing technicians to look up and order parts directly from repair procedures. For UK workshops, this integration is less relevant since AutoZone's physical retail presence is US-focused. However, for US-based workshops, the parts integration provides genuine workflow efficiency.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Who owns ALLDATA?</h3>
<p>ALLDATA LLC is wholly owned by AutoZone, Inc., which acquired the company in 1996. AutoZone is the largest aftermarket auto parts retailer in the US, publicly traded on the NYSE under the ticker AZO.</p>

<h3>Is ALLDATA the same as AutoZone?</h3>
<p>No. ALLDATA is a subsidiary that provides professional repair information software. AutoZone is the parent company that primarily sells aftermarket auto parts to consumers and professionals. They are separate business operations under the same corporate umbrella.</p>
