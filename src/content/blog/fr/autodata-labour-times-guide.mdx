<h2>What Are AutoData Labour Times?</h2>

<p>Labour times — also called "flat rates" or "standard times" — represent the amount of time a competent, qualified technician working at adequate speed should spend on a specific repair operation. They are used for three purposes:</p>

<ol>
<li><strong>Customer quotation</strong> — Calculating the labour portion of a repair estimate</li>
<li><strong>Technician payment</strong> — Bonus/flat rate pay schemes where technicians are paid per hour of "clocked" work completed</li>
<li><strong>Efficiency benchmarking</strong> — Comparing actual time to complete against the standard</li>
</ol>

<p>AutoData labour times are the UK market reference for European vehicles. They are used directly by major workshop management systems including Garage Hive, Autowork Online, and many dealer management systems.</p>

<h3>Understanding the Time Format</h3>
<p>AutoData labour times are expressed in hours and decimals, not hours and minutes:</p>
<ul>
<li>3.0 hours = 3 hours 0 minutes</li>
<li>3.5 hours = 3 hours 30 minutes</li>
<li>3.4 hours = 3 hours 24 minutes</li>
<li>0.8 hours = 48 minutes</li>
</ul>

<p>At your workshop's hourly rate (e.g., £80/hour), a 3.4-hour operation bills at £272 in labour.</p>

<h2>What's Included (and What's Not) in AutoData Labour Times</h2>

<p>Understanding what the stated time includes and excludes prevents underquoting significantly:</p>

<h3>Typically Included</h3>
<ul>
<li>Normal vehicle preparation (raising on ramp, removing undertray shields)</li>
<li>All steps of the specific repair operation</li>
<li>Normal refitting and adjustment</li>
</ul>

<h3>Typically NOT Included (quote separately)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Brake fluid bleeding after brake system work</li>
<li>Coolant filling and purging after cooling system work</li>
<li>Oil and filter change bundled with engine work</li>
<li>Ancillary work that must be removed to access the repair (e.g., removing engine mounts for timing belt access on some vehicles)</li>
<li>Seized or corroded fasteners requiring additional time</li>
<li>Diagnostic time</li>
</ul>

<div style={{background:"#e8f5e9", borderLeft:"4px solid #43a047", padding:"16px 20px", borderRadius:"6px", margin:"24px 0"}}>
<strong>💡 Pro Tip</strong><br/>
Always add a minimum of 0.2–0.5 hours to AutoData labour times for "associated operations" — the small tasks that always occur but aren't in the time: cable-tying harnesses back neatly, bleeding a brake circuit, filling and purging coolant, resetting the service light, and a post-repair road test. If you don't add these, you're pricing them as free.
</div>

<h2>Step-by-Step: How to Build an Accurate Job Quote Using AutoData Times</h2>

<ol>
<li><strong>Identify all required operations</strong> — List every repair, service, and associated task needed for the job</li>
<li><strong>Look up each operation in AutoData</strong> — For the specific vehicle make, model, year, and engine</li>
<li><strong>Identify associated operations</strong> — Fluid bleeds, resets, road tests — add time estimates</li>
<li><strong>Check for additional time triggers</strong> — Is the vehicle high mileage? Are there likely seized fasteners? Is access noted as difficult?</li>
<li><strong>Multiply total hours by your labour rate</strong></li>
<li><strong>Add consumables</strong> — Coolant, brake fluid, rags, cleaning fluid — these are rarely zero</li>
<li><strong>Add a 10–15% contingency</strong> — For unknowns on a first-inspection quote</li>
<li><strong>Present as a range if appropriate</strong> — "Between £X and £Y depending on condition" on complex jobs</li>
</ol>

<strong>⚠️ Warning</strong><br/>

A timing belt on a 2018 VW Golf 2.0 TDI takes a very different time from a 2022 VW Tiguan 2.0 TDI — the access, tooling requirements, and associated operations can differ by 2+ hours. Always look up the specific vehicle before quoting.

<h2>Using AutoData Labour Times for Technician Pay</h2>

<p>Flat rate technician pay schemes pay technicians for the number of standard hours they complete, not the number of clock hours they work. A technician who completes a 3.4-hour job in 2.8 actual hours earns 3.4 hours of pay — an incentive for efficiency.</p>

<p>For this system to work fairly, the standard times must be accurate and verifiable. AutoData labour times are the accepted independent reference for this calculation in UK independent workshops — neither the technician nor the workshop owner sets the standard, so disputes are minimised.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Are AutoData labour times too low or too accurate?</h3>
<p>AutoData labour times represent industry-standard efficient completion times. Many workshops quote them directly; others add a percentage for known local factors (high-mileage vehicles, accessible location constraints). They are accurate as a baseline but may need upward adjustment for unusually difficult access or high-mileage vehicles with corrosion complications.</p>

<h3>Does AutoData cover electric vehicle service labour times?</h3>
<p>Yes. AutoData is progressively expanding EV service labour times, covering brake pad replacement, coolant system work, 12V battery replacement, tyre and wheel work, and some HV-adjacent procedures. HV system-specific procedures are typically excluded from standard labour times as they require specialist qualification.</p>

<h3>How does AutoData compare to Solera/real OEM times?</h3>
<p>AutoData (owned by Solera) uses verified data from manufacturer technical data, supplemented by real workshop validation. For European makes, the OEM and AutoData times are typically within 10–15% of each other. AutoData simplifies access significantly compared to individual manufacturer portals.</p>

<h3>Can I access AutoData labour times on my mobile?</h3>
<p>AutoData is accessible via browser on mobile devices, though the optimised experience is on a desktop or tablet for workshop use. AutoFixData's platform interface is mobile-responsive for quick lookups.</p>
