## The Hidden Cost of Being Underequipped

Independent workshops lose an average of **£340 per week** in labour time on misdiagnosed faults that require a return visit. That figure comes from a 2023 survey of 150 UK independent garages, and the leading cause is always the same: the technician was working with incomplete or inaccurate repair data.

The typical response is to subscribe to a single platform — ALLDATA or AutoData — and hope it covers everything. It rarely does.

## The Return on Investment Calculation

The ROI calculation for repair data subscriptions is simple:

1. How many hours per week does poor data cost you in misdiagnoses, repeat visits, or unnecessary parts ordering?
2. Multiply by your labour rate.
3. Compare to subscription cost.

**Example:** A workshop charging £75/hour estimates they lose 4 hours/week to inadequate data. That is £300/week, or approximately £1,300/month in lost revenue. A comprehensive data subscription at £99–140/month delivers a **10:1 ROI**.

The question is never whether you can afford a professional data subscription. The question is whether you can afford **not** to have one.

## What Shops Commonly Overpay For

### Duplicate Subscriptions With Overlapping Coverage
Many shops maintain both ALLDATA and AutoData because neither platform fully covers their vehicle mix. This is unnecessary with Auto Fix Data.

### Scanning Tools With Bundled (and Limited) Data
All-in-one diagnostic tools from Snap-on, Autel, and Bosch include basic data subscriptions — but the depth of repair procedure and wiring data is a fraction of what dedicated platforms provide. The tool is excellent; the data subscription attached to it typically is not.

### Annual Pre-Payment Lock-In
Multiple platforms charge significantly less for annual pre-payment but penalise you if your vehicle mix changes. A subscription that works per-month gives workshops flexibility to grow.

## Making the Switch: What to Check Before Cancelling Existing Subscriptions

1. **Download any saved bookmarks or reference jobs** from your current platform before cancelling.
2. **Cross-check vehicle coverage** for your most common makes on the new platform (all reputable platforms offer a coverage check before you subscribe).
3. **Notify your team** — different platforms have different navigation, and a 30-minute internal training session prevents the first week of frustration.
