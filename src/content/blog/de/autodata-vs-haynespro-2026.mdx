<h2>Platform Overview</h2>

<p><strong>AutoData</strong> (now owned by Solera) has been the European workshop data market leader for over two decades. Its core strength is depth of service, maintenance, and technical data for European vehicle makes — covering everything from oil specification to ADAS calibration procedures to DSG transmission service sequences.</p>

<p><strong>HaynesPro</strong> (Haynes International's professional platform) evolved from the iconic Haynes printed workshop manual brand. Its professional proposition centres on visual clarity — particularly its vivid colour-coded wiring diagrams — combined with a broad international vehicle coverage at competitive pricing.</p>

<h2>European Service Data: Who Wins?</h2>

<p>This is AutoData's home ground, and the gap is significant.</p>

<h3>AutoData European Service Data</h3>
<ul>
<li>Oil change specifications by vehicle and engine variant — including approvals (ACEA, OEM-specific)</li>
<li>Service interval data for all major and minor service points</li>
<li>Brake fluid, coolant, transmission fluid specifications with exact quantities</li>
<li>Service indicator reset procedures (including electronic reset sequences for all major makes)</li>
<li>Timing belt and timing chain service data — step-by-step procedures, locking tool specifications, torque values</li>
<li>DSG, DCT, and automatic transmission fluid change procedures</li>
</ul>

<h3>HaynesPro European Service Data</h3>
<p>HaynesPro provides solid service data for common European makes, covering oil specifications, service intervals, and basic fluid capacities. For mainstream vehicles like VW Golf, BMW 3 Series, and Ford Focus, the data is adequate. For less common models, specialist engine variants, or recent model year updates, coverage can be thinner than AutoData.</p>

<p><strong>Verdict: AutoData wins clearly on depth, particularly for European-spec service data.</strong></p>

<h2>Wiring Diagrams: Who Wins?</h2>

<p>This is much closer, and HaynesPro has a legitimate claim to the top position.</p>

<h3>AutoData Wiring Diagrams</h3>
<p>AutoData provides simplified, colour-coded wiring diagrams that are purpose-designed for rapid circuit tracing. They are accurate and very clear for experienced technicians. Some technicians find them slightly over-simplified compared to full OEM schematics.</p>

<h3>HaynesPro Wiring Diagrams</h3>
<p><a href="https://www.autofixdata.net/haynes-pro">HaynesPro technical data</a> is genuinely regarded as having the most visually clear wiring diagrams in the industry. The full-colour, highly detailed schematics are the easiest to navigate for any experience level, and include comprehensive connector pin-out views and component location references.</p>

<p><strong>Verdict: HaynesPro wins on wiring diagram visual quality. AutoData wins on ADAS-related network topology diagrams.</strong></p>

<h2>ADAS Calibration: Who Wins?</h2>

<p>AutoData leads significantly in this rapidly-growing area.</p>

<p>For ADAS calibration procedures, AutoData provides what independent workshops actually need:</p>
<ul>
<li>Static calibration target specifications (exact distances, heights, lateral offsets) with tolerances</li>
<li>Camera calibration target type requirements (some manufacturers require OEM-branded targets)</li>
<li>Dynamic calibration requirements (speed, distance, road conditions, weather constraints)</li>
<li>Registration of component replacement in control module</li>
</ul>

<p>HaynesPro covers some ADAS calibration steps but lacks the comprehensive target specification data that AutoData provides for both camera and radar systems. This is a meaningful gap — workshops using HaynesPro alone for ADAS calibration may not have the specific target measurements needed for safe, accurate calibration.</p>

<p><strong>Verdict: AutoData wins decisively on ADAS calibration data.</strong></p>

<h2>Labour Times: Who Wins?</h2>

<p>AutoData's labour time database is widely considered the industry reference for UK and European markets. The data is used by major estimating systems and is verified against actual workshop conditions. <a href="https://www.autofixdata.net/autodata">AutoData workshop software</a> labour times are what most UK workshops quote against for job pricing.</p>

<p>HaynesPro includes labour time data but its reputation for depth and accuracy in the UK market is somewhat below AutoData's — particularly for complex jobs involving stripping multiple components to access a particular repair.</p>

<p><strong>Verdict: AutoData wins on labour time depth and market adoption.</strong></p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Is AutoData or HaynesPro better for a UK MOT and service centre?</h3>
<p>AutoData leads for MOT preparation and service work. Its service interval data, fluid specifications, and reset procedures are the most complete for UK-market vehicles. HaynesPro is better used as a wiring diagram supplement for electrical work during service.</p>

<h3>Does AutoData cover US vehicles?</h3>
<p>AutoData's US vehicle coverage exists but is notably thinner than its European data. For significant volumes of US-spec vehicles, ALLDATA remains the better primary platform.</p>

<h3>Which platform is better for timing belt and chain repairs?</h3>
<p>AutoData leads for timing procedure data — locking tool specifications, step sequences, torque values, and belt tension specifications are more comprehensively documented than in HaynesPro for most European makes.</p>
